‘Genesis Responsive Menu: Accessible Edition’

Next up, I want to take a break from the regular format of these updates to bring some attention to an important discussion (and a lot of hard work) that’s been going on in the #accessibility channel of the Genesis Slack group.

First, some context. If you weren’t aware, Genesis 2.2 was released at the end of August and brought about some fantastic accessibility improvements.

Part of these improvements include support for more accessible menus (allowing users to ‘tab’ through menu items and so on).

Now, these features rely on having accessibility ‘switched on’ in the child theme that Genesis is using, but this is a small change in most cases (for more details see Jesse Petersen’s ‘Two Things You Need to Do to Your Genesis 2.2 Theme’ article).

However in some cases, where a child theme includes a specific responsive (mobile) menu and also has accessibility ‘switched on’, there has been a small bug where responsive menu items with sub-menus might ‘flicker’ or behave unexpectedly.

In short, nothing that’s going to bring your site down, so there’s no panic or alarm here, but something that could be improved upon in the child themes where this is the case.

Not to be put off, the crack team of Robin Cornett, Marcy Diaz and Bradley Potter (with contributions from Gary Jones, Ozzy Rodriguez and Rian Rietveld) – and apologies if I missed anyone else – have been hard at work looking at solutions for this and last week (it just missed my update sadly) Robin shared her work in her excellent post:

Genesis Responsive Menu: Accessible Edition

In her post, Robin explains the whole thing way better than me(!) and also provides all the code for an accessible responsive menu solution based on all the discussions and testing in the #accessibility channel.

If you’re a theme developer (or child theme user) and have noticed a responsive menu issue with accessibility switched on in your theme or you’re planning to build a new child theme with a responsive menu I encourage you to check her post.

Having dipped in and out of the discussion on the #accessibility channel of the Genesis Slack group, I can only take my hat off to all those involved and in my mind this is one of the best examples of why the Genesis community has such a fantastic reputation for cooperation and working for the ‘greater good’.
