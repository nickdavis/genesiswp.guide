Remembering Jesse Petersen.

Jesse Petersen, 1978-2017

I’m sure that most, if not all, of you reading this have already heard the sad news about Jesse Petersen’s passing on Wednesday after his brave battle with cystic fibrosis.

It is a mark of the respect and love in which he was held by the community, to see so many tributes to the man on Twitter, Facebook and elsewhere.

I’m not sure what I can add to those, particularly to those who knew him in person.

So I’ll just say that I was lucky enough to know Jesse from the first few months I started playing around with Genesis around five or six years ago.

I had no clue what I was doing, but that even though I was a ‘nobody’ in the community and he was a Genesis contributor, preferred developer and all round Genesis rockstar, he was decent enough to follow me back on Twitter.

I remember the great WordSesh presentation he did in what was the middle of the night for him (on his time zone).

Starting a #GenesisWP tribe with him in Clash of Clans (we were the only two members).

And, most recently, after sending my newsletter every week getting an ‘out of office’ message from Jesse with a clear note in it about the days and hours you should expect a reply from him.

And it always makes me smile because I know how organised he is in his business about setting client expectations and all those other things I should take more care of myself. So even though it was just an out of office message, it was a little bit of Jesse’s personality in my inbox every week.

Out of office message or not, every time I send this weekly newsletter from now on I’ll be thinking of him.

Please help if you can

Jesse was 38 and survived by his wife and two children. As a cystic fibrosis sufferer Jesse was unable to get life insurance so a fund has been set up to help his family.

If you’d like to help Jesse’s family you can do so via the fund.

RIP Jesse, you did everything that you could.

I’m glad to call you a friend.
