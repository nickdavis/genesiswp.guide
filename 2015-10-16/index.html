The best new WordPress Genesis things

This Week in Genesis (16 Oct 2015)

16 October 2015 by Nick Davis


Photo by Minimography

As summer turns to winter (at least on my part of the planet) This Week in Genesis (#22) has got just the thing to warm your bones, including:

  • Brian Gardner and Justin Sternberg sharing Genesis themes on GitHub,
  • the release of a new development framework for Genesis from Dave Dean,
  • plus, Genesis 2.2.3 goes live.

Let’s do this.

Themes

First up this week is a very special treat from Mr Brian Gardner. He’s just released his latest and great personal blogging theme (currently on display at briangardner.com) on GitHub for your downloading (and forking) pleasure.

Fork this — http://t.co/fwPBlqXbD1 #genesiswp

— Brian Gardner (@bgardner) October 13, 2015

And releasing personal themes on GitHub is obviously in vogue this week because Justin Sternberg is at it too, releasing his Genesis photography theme over on GitHub.

Finally put my theme for http://t.co/0JtXVn4gp7 up on github, if you’re interested in that sorta thing: http://t.co/fYCA67K9FZ #GenesisWP

— Justin Sternberg (@Jtsternberg) October 14, 2015

Showcase!

First up this week, a great new site from those awesome folk over at WP Site Care and a great design by Shay Bocks (yes, Shay Bocks of Foodie Pro fame) for pastashoppe.com

Pretty cool eCommerce site the @wpsitecare crew built. Turned out ?! Design by @shaybocks. Fun product too https://t.co/1gzXWWZt3b

— Ryan D. Sullivan (@ryandonsullivan) October 15, 2015

Next up, a great new site from Matthew Snider for, erm, matthewsnider.co

#genesiswp my new site has launched! http://t.co/sJORkvrI0P

— Matthew Snider (@matthewfsnider) October 13, 2015

I have to give a shout out to this one. The team over at The DMA have done a great job with this new blog for blog.csiro.au

We're very proud of this #WordPress project: a new site for @csironews – check it out here >> https://t.co/8fsikH4RHI #genesiswp

— The DMA (@the_dma) October 12, 2015

Thauna over at BellanoStudio shares sunbp.co

Just launched a new site for Sunbelt Prop. (http://t.co/aq4y8y6GJO) lots of cool features made w/ love & #genesiswp pic.twitter.com/H4ylDzh8hy

— Thauna (@BellanoStudio) October 12, 2015

Jo Waltham launches a new site for houseofelliotcollection.com

Delight to announce the launch of a new website for @Couture_boots http://t.co/JN6jzDHS7q Custom #genesiswp theme & WooCommerce

— Callia Web (@CalliaWeb) October 12, 2015

And finally, it probably wouldn’t be a showcase round-up without a new site from this lady and this week is no exception, with Jackie D’Elia releasing a new website for buchanansplants.com

My latest project sports a fresh look for Buchanan’s Native Plants http://t.co/XqCKfLv0u4 #GenesisWP #WordPress pic.twitter.com/ITJexF77Au

— Jackie D'Elia (@SavvyJackie) October 9, 2015

Podcasts

This week on the Office Hours Podcast, Carrie Dils caught up with the impressive Rachel Baker. Not only is Rachel a WordPress Core Contributor but she’s also a lead dev on the WP REST API which promises to bring a whole host of exciting new things to WordPress in the near future.

Direct Youtube link for today's #officefm https://t.co/JUJEx3W9hA

— Davinder Singh (@idavinder) October 15, 2015

While over on the UK Genesis podcast next week you can catch Dave Dean (aka @wpmusketeer) on Wednesday 21 October at 2pm (UK time, of course).

I'm being interviewed on the @UKGenesis Podcast next week http://t.co/eQ7lPVca2M #genesiswp #ukgenesis Wish me luck!

— Dave Dean (@wpmusketeer) October 15, 2015

Best of the rest

Genesis 2.2.3 is released with a few accessibility and other fixes, check out the changelog.

Genesis 2.2.3 now available — update now! Here’s the changelog — http://t.co/819oIzwOJn #genesiswp

— Brian Gardner (@bgardner) October 12, 2015

A top article, as ever, from Bill Erickson on H1 tags on front page with Genesis (h/t Seth Spears).

h1 tags on front page with #Genesiswp: http://t.co/DPxCAYldAs via @BillErickson

— Seth Spears (@SpearsMarketing) October 13, 2015

Jesse hints at new Genesis courses.

I've been informed that @treehouse is not pursuing any new #genesiswp courses so I will be creating my own. Stay tuned. #wordpress

— Jesse Ⓦ Petersen (@jpetersen) October 13, 2015

Rafal Tomal shares a tutorial of how he created his recent Journal theme.

I shared a tutorial on how I coded the Journal Theme for #genesiswp. Check it out:   http://t.co/7ulg6zk1LG

— Rafal Tomal (@RafalTomal) October 15, 2015

Next up, one for all you developers out there. That clever chap Dave Dean has just released his new development framework for WordPress entitled, Musket Theme, and it looks pretty neat. Right now, it’s available for £80 / €100 / $120 (for a one year support license) but I imagine that’s a whole lot of hours saved if you get into the habit of coding with it regularly.

It's finally released! Based on @genesiscampwp talk – Musket http://t.co/mnYdzczDtn #genesiswp #wordpress dev framework

— Dave Dean (@wpmusketeer) October 15, 2015

If you’re a Genesis fan from the UK (or even further afield) you might want to check out this potential UK Genesis meet-up.

A week in March. A UK Centre Parks location. With all your #genesiswp pals? Interested? Tell us what you'd want http://t.co/mJOfu7kfJJ

— Jo Waltham (@JoWaltham) October 15, 2015

And finally, if you appreciated his work in the showcase this week, you can now hire Matthew Snider for your project too (but you’d better be quick).

I have a spot open for any #genesiswp work. Hit me up if you need help! #wordpress #design

— Matthew Snider (@matthewfsnider) October 15, 2015

That’s all for this week.

Au revoir et bon weekend.
