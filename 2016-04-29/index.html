This Week in Genesis (29 Apr 2016)

29 April 2016 by Nick Davis


Photo by SplitShire

This Week in Genesis we’re celebrating turning one; launching a new feature right here on GenesisWP.guide; oh and we’re still rounding up everything else that’s been going on this week as well, including lots of new themes, an amazing showcase, and a popular new plugin and tutorials.

But, just for once, I hope you’ll forgive me talking about this website first.

One year of GenesisWP.guide!

Launching a new website to share all the #genesiswp things http://t.co/2mgV0dpgAw

— Nick Davis (@iamnickdavis) April 24, 2015

This week we’re (I’m) celebrating one year of GenesisWP.guide. I know most of you expected me to give up after three weeks (and in truth, so did I) but somehow we’re still here – with only a few editions missed along the way – and reaching more and more people every week. (If you’re nostalgic check out the first introductory post and the first This Week in Genesis).

Thank you, thank you, thank you

Of course we wouldn’t have made it to one year though without all of you reading, sharing, commenting on and subscribing to this, so first of all a huge thank you to you, yes, you, reading this.

You are awesome.

But I also have to thank:

  • all the guest editors so far (Tara, Tony, Dhishna, Jason and Sandee);
  • all my interview guests;
  • Calvin Koepke, especially for his encouragement in the early days;
  • Carrie Dils, for having me on her podcast;
  • Brian Gardner for his continued all-round awesomeness and support;

  • my regular retweeters, especially Bradley, Marcy, Andrea and Andy;
  • and, most recently, Tonya Mork, Vaughan Simons, Jackie D’Elia, and too many others to mention, just for their all round support and encouragement.

Every time someone comments or shares or subscribes or writes me an email I’m over the moon.

…and something new (Listings!)

Listings! Introducing something new for #genesiswp on https://t.co/0S8EJsTFsA pic.twitter.com/q782AzQ6Ml

— GenesisWPguide (@GenesisWPguide) April 29, 2016

And today, I’m also really excited to launch a new feature, which I’ve dubbed Listings. In short, it’s like This Week in Genesis but everyday! And the best part is you can post your own Genesis stuff (or other people’s cool stuff) and upvote what you like.

It’s still very beta (there’s a LOT more I want to add) but, if you’re a creator of Genesis stuff (whether it’s a theme, plugin, showcase entry or just an awesome Genesis blog post) I hope this will help you get a lot more eyeballs on whatever you want to share with the Genesis community.

Or, if you just want to keep up with what’s happening every day and don’t have a lot of time, you can quickly check in and see the best stuff as voted for by your peers either for today or for the previous week.

‘That’s awesome, but why?’

Because I’ve never wanted this site to be just about my opinion of what is good – and I love having guest editors has been – but this way everyone can have their say, even if they only have a minute or two.

And yes, what’s popular on the listings will definitely influence what I’m feature here on This Week in Genesis and what I share on Facebook and Twitter, so if you’ve got something to share don’t be shy about posting it. This community is creating awesome stuff and I want to do whatever I can to help you succeed.

If my weekly updates have been helpful (or entertaining?) to you over the last year, I’d love it if you check out the Listings on the homepage of GenesisWP.guide, register (if you feel like upvoting or posting something, it’s quick and painless I promise) and, of course, let me know what you love or hate about it so I can make it better.
