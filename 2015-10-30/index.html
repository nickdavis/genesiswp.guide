The best new WordPress Genesis things

This Week in Genesis (30 Oct 2015)

30 October 2015 by Nick Davis


Photo by Unsplash

This Week in Genesis (#24) there’s new themes (including the release of Atmosphere Pro), a tease of the forthcoming No Sidebar theme from Brian Gardner, a very crowded Genesis related podcast; and your chance to send questions to Rafal Tomal about his design process.

Let’s go!

Themes

Atmosphere is here!

That’s right the eagerly anticipated Atmosphere Pro theme from Brian Gardner is now live and available from the good people over at StudioPress.

I’m a big fan of this one, so check it out.

Introducing Atmosphere, a theme I designed personally for you — https://t.co/HwKrOQ4L5a #genesiswp

— Brian Gardner (@bgardner) October 28, 2015

And if that wasn’t enough, he’s already working on a No Sidebar theme (inspired by the design of his No Sidebar project) for StudioPress as well, as he reveals in a dribbble sneak peek.

Yep, I started the No Sidebar theme — https://t.co/rXmHNn22OZ #genesiswp

— Brian Gardner (@bgardner) October 27, 2015

There’s another Genesis theme out this week, Captivate by Shivanand Sharma, and he’s doing a giveaway too…

Win Flat UI WordPress Genesis child theme "Captivate" for no cost! https://t.co/w5PhLUDm9X #genesiswp

— Shivanand Sharma (@ShivanandWP) October 29, 2015

And finally in themes, there’s a free Genesis theme release by Gaurav Pareek, Anahita.

New #genesiswp child theme 😀 https://t.co/JkhrAx0Tfq

— Gaurav Pareek (@grvruulz) October 30, 2015

Showcase

First up in the showcase this week, the slick design for DigitalCommerce.com (the latest project from Rainmaker) by Rainmaker lead designer, Rafal Tomal.

Check out my newest design: https://t.co/7eG2OScAgd – built completely on #genesiswp and https://t.co/IRH42zG8RH.

— Rafal Tomal (@RafalTomal) October 26, 2015

…a great one page design from JT Grauke for jtgrauke.com (h/t Brian Gardner).

Wanna know how to slay a one-page design? Here ya go —> https://t.co/JsyU5uZxas props @jtgrauke

— Brian Gardner (@bgardner) October 27, 2015

…the fine folks over at Design Palette Pro (the WordPress plugin that lets you easily customise the design of your StudioPress themes) redesign their site – genesisdesignpro.com – for extra awesome.

oh hey. we redesigned the entire site to make it better and easier to use. take a look! https://t.co/T3GUbr8wPN

— Design Palette Pro (@DesignPalette) October 28, 2015

…great new design by Claire J De Boer for thegiftofwriting.com (h/t Brian Gardner).

Hey @brianclark — wanna see a brilliant design on Rainmaker? Check out what @ClaireJDeBoer did — https://t.co/QpeQOqaYy7

— Brian Gardner (@bgardner) October 28, 2015

And finally, I forced one more showcase entry out of Brian Bourn last week but it was too late to include. So without further ado, check out the excellent cindyratzlaff.com from Bourn Creative!

@nickcdavis Thank you! One more that launched late yesterday: https://t.co/CkcwBVoMKy

— Brian Bourn (@brianbourn) October 23, 2015

Podcasts

First up this week, something different, a whole host of Genesis folk over on the WP Crowd podcast talking Genesis and why you should use it.

Miss our #genesiswp show today? Fear not! Its on Youtube! Learn about the best theme framework for #WordPress https://t.co/hUnfd2m8UJ

— The WP Crowd (@thewpcrowd) October 28, 2015

Next up, the ever awesome Carrie Dils chats with James Laws and Kevin Stover of WP Ninjas fame about Ninja Forms and Roman military history over on the Office Hours podcast.

Just finished a great interview with @jameslaws & @kstover of @wpninjas! Catch the replay here if you missed it: https://t.co/qsmrIgrZky

— Office Hours Podcast (@officehoursfm) October 29, 2015

And finally, the UK Genesis podcast will be back on Wednesday 4 November (live at 2pm UK time) with a chat with Dom Wint.

Next week's guest is @CleanPageDesign: https://t.co/wMIW2326Pn #genesiswp pic.twitter.com/7AVixgWPm1

— UK Genesis (@UKGenesis) October 28, 2015

Best of the rest

Rafal Tomal is planning to write a case study on his new designs for RainmakerPlatform.com and DigitalCommerce.com and he’d love to have your questions or suggestions.

I’m planning to write a case study on https://t.co/RHHauDbyBU and https://t.co/7eG2OScAgd designs. Any questions or suggestions? #genesiswp

— Rafal Tomal (@RafalTomal) October 28, 2015

I’ve been digging Minimography (the royalty-free minimalist photos project from Brian Gardner and Lauren Mancke) a lot recently, including featuring some of their photos on this blog and now there’s even more photos to choose from.

New (FREE) photos available at Minimography — https://t.co/Q8IYM5fxWg

— Brian Gardner (@bgardner) October 27, 2015

Those lovely people over at Design Palette Pro haven’t just been updating their site design (see this week’s Showcase), their super smart plugin is now supporting design tweaks of StudioPress themes Altitude Pro and Author Pro too…

hey y’all! a new release of Design Palette just hit the airwaves! Altitude Pro and Author Pro included! https://t.co/MZzuZ29gOG

— Design Palette Pro (@DesignPalette) October 29, 2015

Are you a designer? Would you like to win a dribbble invite? Submit your best blog design to Calvin Koepke pronto for your chance to win, plus he’ll even code the winning entry into a theme for you (to be released on GitHub). A fantastic chance to have your work seen by a wider audience.

Tomorrow is the LAST day to submit your design for a free @Dribbble invite — https://t.co/MuwDO83Osg #genesiswp pic.twitter.com/3JVqw21v4h

— Calvin Koepke (@cjkoepke) October 30, 2015

And finally…

Not to turn it into Brian Gardner week (hey, I didn’t include ‘that’ photo) if you’re curious to learn a little more about the StudioPress founder and what makes him tick, check out this great chat he did with Jon Nastor over on the Hack the Entrepreneur podcast.

Brian Gardner on ‘Hitting the Wall’ and Pushing Through https://t.co/vlPCsrTGWY pic.twitter.com/EyQapFAByq

— Jonny Nastor (@jonnastor) October 28, 2015

That’s it!

Till next week, big love and keep on keeping on.

PS If you didn’t already, check out my interview with Dave Dean (aka @wpmusketeer) plus he’s giving away a copy of Musket, his new Genesis development framework.
